Mapping How The United States Generates Its Electricity

Visualization

Image source: Mapping how the United States generates its electricity (The Washington Post)

Description

The visualization shown in the webpage above clearly illustrates the source of the power plants in the U.S. Each point in the plot displays the location of a power plant. Its size represents the output of the plant and the color stands for the source of the power.

One can easily find that each region/state in the country has a certain characteristics in its primary source of the power. For example, coal-based power is widely used in east side of the country, where there are a lot of mining sites of the resource beside the mountains. On the other hand, solar power plant is a popular plant type in the southwest region, in which people can except more shiny days in a year than any of other regions. The flat land and stable weather is a good condition for wind-based power so that there are many wind power plants in the plain area. Also, users may find which part of the country has higher output of electricity.

Additionally, the webpage offers an interactive bar plot, with which users can explore the component of the power in each state. Users may click the type of the source to rearrange the bar plot and hover the section of the plot to display the share of the source in the state. This helps them further understand the primary source of the energy they use everyday in various locations of the country.

In short, this set of the visualizations offers a good user experience. It has a clear motivation of the visualization (i.e., to display the distribution of various power plants), appropriate selection of size/color of each point and legend placement, and additional feature that enables users to further investigate the details of the topic. It might be further better visualization if it could let users select the resource type or output power to dynamically change the plot on the map.

Extra: How Long Would It Take To Travel From Tokyo?

Visualization

Image source: リニアは日本をどれだけ狭くするのか？ ～到達所要時間ビジュアライゼーションマップに挑戦～ (Yahoo! JAPAN)

Description

We report this as an extra visualization as it is not described in English. This visualization presented by a big-data analysis team in Yahoo! JAPAN illustrates the required travel time from Tokyo station to any location in Japan by public transit. They have completed this project with the big data provided by their navigation app. The details will be found in this video. The color in the visualization represents the travel time from Tokyo station (red: short - green: long). As it targets only the public transit, some locations could not be reached out.

In this visualization, one can notice that the high speed rail network clearly contributes to shorten the travel time when it is available. The following image is a capture from the video. It indicates that traveling from Tokyo to Nagoya, which is approximately 220 miles away, is as quick as travelling to Kofu, which is less than 80 miles from Tokyo. Consequently, the map has some "veins" by the high speed rails from Tokyo station as shown in the first image.

Additionally, the research team has tried to simulate the same plot after the completion of the maglev line that will be available in 2040. The video is available here. The top-left plot in the image above is the visualization before the maglev, and the bottom-right one is after the maglev. Clearly, the new line not only extends the furthest places where people can travel from Tokyo within 3 hours, but also introduce new locations which will acquire an access to a high-speed transit system.

Although it is a static visualization and users cannot manipulate the plot interactively, this work is an exemplar of a geolocation-based big data. With this video, the general public can easily understand the power of the current public transit system and the benefits of the brand new transportation technology.
